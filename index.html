About Me

I am currently enrolled in the Joint Bachelor’s Degree Program between Columbia University and the City University of Hong Kong. At Columbia I major in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science (CGPA 4.0970/4.00) and at CityU I follow the Artificial Intelligence stream with a minor in Mathematics (CGPA 3.98/4.3). My coursework covers advanced programming, natural language processing, deep learning for computer vision, computer science theory, stochastic processes and optimization.

My research interests lie in sample–efficient reinforcement learning and efficient deep learning for edge AI. At Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute I am developing a learning–history filtering (LHF) mechanism that mitigates suboptimal trajectories in in–context RL. Earlier at the Shenzhen Research Institute, CityU, I designed a reinforcement–learning scheduler that coordinates CUDA streams and pruned CNN/Transformer models for real–time inference on edge GPUs. I also placed within the top 15% of the Yale/UNCCH Geophysical Waveform Inversion Kaggle competition by building a physics–guided ML pipeline.

Outside the lab I served as a student helper for web development coursework and led a team in the International Collegiate Programming Contest. Explore the Publications, Research Experience, Academic Experience and Awards pages for more details about my work.
